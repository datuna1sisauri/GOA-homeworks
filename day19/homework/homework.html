Most basic information about football

Association football, more commonly known as fottball or soccer,
is a team sport played between two teams of 11 players each,

who almost exclusively use their feet to propel a ball around a rectangular field called a pitch.

The objective of the game is to score more goals than the opposing team by moving the
ball beyond the goal line into a rectangular-framed goal defended by the opposing team.

Traditionally, the game has been played over two 45-minute halves, for a total match time of 90 minutes.

With an estimated 250 million players active in over 200 countries and territories, it is the world's most popular sport.


laws in football

The game of association football is played in accordance with the Laws of the Game, a set of rules that has been in effect since 1863 and maintained by the IFAB since 1886.

The game is played with a football that is 68-70 cm (27-28 in) in circumference. The two teams compete to score goals by getting the ball into the other team's goal

When the ball is in play, the players mainly use their feet, but may also use any other part of their body, such as

  • head
  • shoudler
  • chest

to make long story short they can use all part of their body but can not use hands and arms

Only the goalkeepers may use their hands and arms, and that only within the penalty area

The team that has scored more goals at the end of the game is the winner.

here are situations where a goal can be disallowed, such as an offside call or a foul in the build-up to the goal.
